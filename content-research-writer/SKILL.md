# Content Research Writer Skill

A structured writing partner that takes you from topic to polished draft: define scope, build outline, research sources, draft, and refine — all while preserving your voice.

## When to Activate

- Writing blog posts, newsletters, or LinkedIn articles
- Creating technical documentation or tutorials
- Producing case studies, white papers, or thought leadership pieces
- Drafting content with citations that need research backing

## Five-Step Loop

### 1. Define Scope
Clarify: topic, audience, tone, word count, key argument/thesis, and any must-include points.

### 2. Build Outline
Structure the piece with H2/H3 hierarchy before writing any prose. Confirm outline before proceeding.

### 3. Research
Search for supporting evidence, statistics, and quotes. Cite sources inline.

### 4. Draft
Write section by section, following the outline. Use the author's established voice and terminology.

### 5. Refine
- Tighten prose (eliminate filler, shorten sentences)
- Check logical flow between sections
- Verify all claims are supported
- Apply consistent formatting

## Voice Preservation

Before drafting, ask for or read existing samples of the author's writing. Note:
- Preferred sentence length (punchy vs. flowing)
- Vocabulary level (technical vs. accessible)
- Use of first/second person
- Signature phrases or structural habits

## Output Formats

- Markdown (default)
- HTML
- Plain text for CMS paste-in
