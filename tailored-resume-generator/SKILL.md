# Tailored Resume Generator Skill

Analyze a job description and rewrite or reorganize a resume to maximize relevance, ATS keyword matching, and interview conversion — without fabricating anything.

## When to Activate

- Applying to a specific job and wanting a tailored version of your resume
- Refreshing a resume that isn't getting responses
- Career transition requiring reframing of existing experience
- Generating multiple targeted versions from one master resume

## What You Need to Provide

1. **Job description** — paste the full posting or provide URL
2. **Your background** — existing resume, work history, or bullet list of experience, skills, education

## Five-Step Process

### 1. Job Analysis
Extract from the posting:
- Required skills (hard + soft)
- Preferred qualifications
- Keywords that appear multiple times
- What the employer clearly values most

### 2. Experience Mapping
Map your experience to job requirements:
- Direct matches → lead with these
- Partial matches → reframe to emphasize relevant aspect
- Gaps → identify what to acknowledge vs. omit

### 3. Content Rewriting
- Reorder bullets to lead with most relevant achievements
- Use action verbs that match the job's language
- Quantify achievements: "improved X by Y%" where possible
- Mirror key phrases from the job description naturally

### 4. ATS Optimization
- Include exact keywords from the job posting (not synonyms)
- Avoid tables, columns, headers/footers (confuse parsers)
- Use standard section names: Experience, Education, Skills
- Spell out acronyms at least once

### 5. Output
Clean Markdown resume ready to paste into a Word/Google Doc template.

## Special Cases

- **Career transition**: emphasize transferable skills, lead with a strong summary
- **Recent graduate**: internships, projects, and coursework take priority
- **Senior executive**: board-level impact, P&L responsibility, team scale
- **Technical role**: skills section near top, GitHub/portfolio link prominent

## Ethical Guidelines

- Never add experience, skills, or credentials you don't have
- Reframing is acceptable; fabrication is not
- Flag gaps honestly rather than hiding them
